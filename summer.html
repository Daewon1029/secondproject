Summer Olympic

The Summer Olympic Games, also known as the Summer Olympics or the Games of the Olympiad, is a major international multi-sport event held once every four years.

The inaugural Games took place in 1896 in Athens, Greece, and the most recent was held in 2024 in Paris, France. These Games bring together athletes from around the world to compete in various sports, fostering international unity and sportsmanship.

6 Main Sports

BaseBall

Baseball is a bat-and-ball sport played between two teams of nine players each, where the objective is to score runs by hitting a ball and running around a series of bases.

FootBall

Soccer, also known as football, is a popular sport that involves two teams of 11 players each trying to score goals by using their feet.

Athletics

Running, jumping, and throwing competitions. Highest medal-contested events.

Gymnastics

Gymnastics is contested at the Summer Olympics, including artistic, rhythmic, and trampoline disciplines.

Swimming

Swimming has been a sport at every modern Summer Olympics. It has been open to women since 1912. At the Olympics, swimming has the second-highest number of medal-contested events after athletics.

BasketBall

Basketball is a team sport played on a rectangular court, where two opposing teams of five players each attempt to score points by shooting the ball through the opponent's hoop.
