What is Paralympics?

The Paralympics are major international multi-sport events for athletes with disabilities, held shortly after the Olympic Games and governed by the International Paralympic Committee (IPC).

The Paralympic Games currently feature 29 sports: 23 Summer and 6 Winter sports.

6 Main Sports

Para Badminton

Para badminton is the fastest racket sport at the Paralympics where players compete in singles and doubles events hitting a shuttlecock over the net without return to score points. Players can catch opponents with clear shots, drop shots, lift shots or smashes.

Para Swimming

Para swimming is competitive swimming for individuals with physical, visual, and intellectual impairments, featuring events like backstroke, breaststroke, butterfly, and freestyle.

Para Athetic

Para-athletics is the sport of athletics practiced by people with disabilities, including physical, intellectual, and vision impairments

Para Alpine Skiing

Para alpine skiing evolved from the efforts of veterans with disabilities in Germany and Austria following WWII.

Para Football

Para Football is a foundation and worldwide body of football for persons with disabilities.

Para Ice Hockey

Para ice hockey is a fast-paced, physical sport played by male and female athletes with a physical impairment in the lower part of the body.
