Winter Olympic

The Winter Olympic Games are a major international multi-sport event held every four years for sports practiced on snow and ice.

They are a celebration of winter sports excellence, showcasing athletes from around the world.

6 Main Sports

Figure Skating

An ice sport featuring singles, pairs, and ice dance events. Skaters perform jumps, spins, and choreographed routines judged on technical skill and artistry.

Ice Hockey

A fast-paced team sport played on ice, where two teams compete to score goals using sticks and a puck. Both men’s and women’s tournaments are included.

Nordic Combined

A combination of ski jumping and cross-country skiing. Athletes’ ski jumping results determine their starting positions for the cross-country race.

Ski Jumping

Athletes launch off large ramps to achieve maximum distance and style points. Both men and women compete, with normal and large hill events.

Speed Skating

Long-track ice racing over distances ranging from 500 meters to 10,000 meters. Athletes compete individually or in team pursuits. These six sports form the traditional core of the Winter Olympics, representing both ice and snow disciplines that have defined the Games since their inception.

Cross-Country Skiing

A Nordic sport where athletes race over long distances using classic or freestyle techniques. It tests endurance and speed across varied terrain.
